# Character

## Short description

Nine years old, runs the Crater Lane bakery her grandmother left her.
Competent hands, worried head. Smells faintly of toasted flour.

## Want

To bake festival buns that make the square smell like Grandmother's kitchen —
proof the bakery is in good hands.

## Fear

That one visible mistake will show everyone she's a kid playing baker.

## Contradiction

She tells customers "a lumpy bun is still a bun" — and throws out her own
lumpy ones before anyone sees.

## Secret

She has never actually finished reading Grandmother's recipe book. She's
scared of the last page.

## Voice notes

Short sentences when nervous. Talks to dough like it's a shy customer:
"There you go. No rush."

## Physical anchors

Flour handprint on the left hip of her apron; a checklist folded in her boot;
one eyebrow raised when Crumb starts beeping.

## Relationships

Crumb — best friend, sorter of everything, keeper of her spare courage.
Grandmother — gone, but present in the oven, the recipes, the smell.

## Recurring behaviors

Counts to three before opening the oven door. Taps the counter twice for luck.

## Arc

From "it must be exactly Grandmother's way" to "it must be warm and it must
be shared — the rest is mine to invent."

## Established facts

- Age nine; the bakery has been hers for one year.
- She has run the bakery alone (with Crumb) since Grandmother left it to her.

## Open questions

- What is on the last page of the recipe book?
