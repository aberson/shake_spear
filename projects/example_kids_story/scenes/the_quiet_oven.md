# Scene

## Purpose in story

Opening. Establish the bakery, the friendship, and the festival deadline —
then land the problem: the moon-sugar shipment hasn't come.

## External goal

Nova wants to start the festival prep list: wake the oven, check the pantry,
count the jars.

## Internal/emotional goal

To feel like a real baker on the biggest morning of her year.

## Conflict

The delivery chute is empty. Crumb has checked three times. No jars, no
straw, no note.

## Complication

The oven is already warm and the dough is already rising — the morning can't
be paused while they wait.

## Emotional turn

From cozy routine confidence to a cold-stomach drop — and then Nova's small,
stubborn decision to keep the oven lit anyway.

## Sensory anchors

The oven door's rising squeak; the hum you feel in your teeth; the too-loud
sound of an empty delivery chute flap.

## Key object or image

One clay jar of moon-sugar alone on the pantry shelf, glowing faintly.

## Five beats

1. Entry: Nova counts to three, opens the squeaky oven door, morning begins.
2. Escalation: Crumb reports, mitt raised: "Chute empty. Beep?"
3. Complication: they re-check together — straw dust, no jars; festival is tomorrow.
4. Turn: Nova finds the last jar and holds it up to the dark like a lantern.
5. Exit image: she sets it on the counter and taps the wood twice. "Then we bake smart."
