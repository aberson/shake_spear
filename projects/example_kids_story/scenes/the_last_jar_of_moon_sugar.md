# Scene

## Purpose in story

Midpoint. One jar, one hundred festival buns. Nova must choose between one
perfect glowing batch or trying something no recipe allows.

## External goal

Divide the last jar so every festival bun gets at least a little glow.

## Internal/emotional goal

To stop hearing "that's not how Grandmother did it" in her own head.

## Conflict

The math doesn't work: one jar sweetens one batch, and the square expects ten.
Grandmother's book has no page for "not enough."

## Complication

Crumb, trying to help, has already alphabetized the pantry — and admits he
has something behind his chest panel that isn't in any inventory: his crumb
tin, one crumb from every batch she ever baked.

## Emotional turn

From "there isn't enough" to "there's enough of something else" — the batches
she's already baked are proof she can invent one more.

## Sensory anchors

Sleigh-bell sound of poured sugar; straw dust in the pantry; the click of
Crumb's chest panel opening.

## Key object or image

Crumb's crumb tin, open on the counter next to the last glowing jar.

## Five beats

1. Entry: Nova lines up ten empty batch trays and one small jar.
2. Escalation: the arithmetic fails out loud, tray by tray.
3. Complication: Crumb opens his chest panel — the tin, the whole year of batches.
4. Turn: Nova reads the dates like a recipe book of her own making.
5. Exit image: she pours the jar into the biggest bowl and says, "New page."
