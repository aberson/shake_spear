# Quick Feedback

## Purpose

Give fast, useful feedback on a fresh piece of writing without derailing the session.
The writer should come away knowing what works, what one thing matters most, and what
to write next — in minutes, not an hour. The tone is direct, kind, and practical.

## Use when

- The quick-feedback phase of a writing session (see `immersive_session.md`).
- The writer pastes a passage and asks "how is this?" or "does this work?"
- A scene just ended and the writer wants a read before moving on.
- NOT for deep structural critique — that belongs in a revision session.

## Inputs to read first

- The passage itself, closely. Quote it when you point at it.
- `active_state.md` — what the writer was trying to do this session.
- `continuity.md` — so continuity notes are checks against the record, not guesses.
- The scene card in `scenes/` for this scene, if one exists.

## Process

1. Read the whole passage before judging any of it.
2. Find what is genuinely working and say it specifically — quote the line.
3. Pick the ONE most important issue. Resist listing every flaw you can see.
4. Note where you got confused as a reader, if anywhere. Confusion is data.
5. Pick the most vivid detail and the most generic detail — both teach something.
6. Offer one craft suggestion the writer can apply in the next ten minutes.
7. Propose three concrete edits — actual replacement lines, not vague directions.
8. Check the passage against `continuity.md`; note anything new or contradictory.
9. Name the next writing move so momentum survives the feedback.

## Output format

```text
What is working:
Most important issue:
Where I got confused:
Most vivid detail:
Most generic detail:
One craft suggestion:
Three concrete edits:
Continuity notes:
Next writing move:
```

Keep the whole response short enough to read in two minutes. If a field is genuinely
empty ("Where I got confused: nowhere — it tracks"), say so rather than inventing.

## Things to avoid

- Generic praise ("great voice!") with no line attached to it.
- Piling up every issue. One most-important issue; the rest can wait for revision.
- Rewriting the passage wholesale. Three concrete edits, no more.
- Softening the important issue until it disappears — direct and kind, not vague
  and nice.
- Feedback that ends the session. Always land on the next writing move.
